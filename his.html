Mexican History

Mexico is a country with a rich History, being the third largest country in Latin America and having history that traces back all the way back to pre-colonizational periods.

Its Beagnnings

Pre-Columbian Period

The history of Mexico begins with the indigenous peoples who inhabited the region long before the arrival of Europeans. These civilizations include the Olmec, Maya, Zapotec, and Aztec cultures, which flourished from about 1200 BCE until the arrival of the Spanish in the 16th century.

Colonial Period

In 1519, the Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés arrived in Mexico and began the process of colonization. The Aztec Empire was conquered in 1521, and Mexico became a Spanish colony known as New Spain. Over the next three centuries, the Spanish imposed their language, culture, and religion on the indigenous peoples of Mexico.

The Middle

Independece Movement

In 1810, Father Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla launched a rebellion against the Spanish, sparking the Mexican War of Independence. The war lasted for over a decade and ended in 1821 with the signing of the Treaty of Córdoba, which granted Mexico its independence.

Post-Independece Period

After gaining independence, Mexico went through a period of political instability, with a series of coups and changes in government. In 1846, Mexico went to war with the United States over the annexation of Texas, which Mexico had previously claimed as part of its territory. The war ended in 1848 with the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo, which ceded much of Mexico's northern territory to the United States.

Recent History

Revolution Period

In 1910, the Mexican Revolution began as a rebellion against the Díaz dictatorship. The revolution was led by a diverse group of rebels, including Emiliano Zapata, Pancho Villa, and Venustiano Carranza. The war lasted for almost a decade and resulted in the establishment of a new constitution and a more democratic government.

Modern Mexico

In the years since the revolution, Mexico has undergone many changes and challenges, including economic struggles, political corruption, and a long-running conflict with drug cartels. Today, Mexico is a federal republic with a presidential system of government, and its economy is one of the largest in the world. Mexican culture and traditions continue to be a source of pride and identity for its people.
